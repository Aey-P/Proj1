  This project, developed from September 2024 to April 2025, aims to develop a radiation transmission technique to be utilized in industrial quality control processes and in-process measurements.

  In the beginning, we developed this project for our microcontroller project. The process involves a 1x1 NaI detector that detects radiation and sends an electric signal to the General Purpose Scaler, LUDLUM M2000, for signal processing. Then, the square wave output is directed to our developed liquid volume measuring equipment (LVM) for counting, calibration, and calculations of the liquid quantity in small containments such as soft drink cans.

  In this project, we utilize a transmission technique to measure water level and volume. Our work is divided into two parts: the Electronic Part and the Mechanical Part.

Electronic Part

  We have developed several electronic boards, including Amplifier, Single Channel Analysis(SCA), High voltage powersupply(HV), and Low voltage power supply

  Our HV board currently designed for use with the Eberline 2x2 NaI detector and a Cs-137 source, which operates at approximately 1000 V. However, this high-voltage setting can be adjusted directly on the board to accommodate different detectors, ensuring versatility in application.

For our Arduino code, we divided it into 4 operation modes.

Mechanic Part

  We organized our electronic boards inside a metal box for easy inspection. Therefore, we discovered that our high voltage generates the most noise among all of the boards, whether the source of the noise is from the MOSFET or the transformer. To address this, we later placed the high-voltage board as far away from the amplifier board as possible. Additionally, we created a filter board and applied aluminum tape to help reduce the noise.

  We also construct a framework to securely hold all components of the system. This includes using an angle iron to support our electronic box, a 200-liter tank, a radiation source (Cs-137), and a 2x2 NaI detector.
